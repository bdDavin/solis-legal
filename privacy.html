Beam — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Beam is published by Ben Davin (the "developer"). This Privacy Policy describes how Beam handles your information.

What Beam doesn't do

Beam has no servers of its own, no user accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs. The developer does not collect, transmit, sell, share, or store any personal data about you.

Apple-provided services

To work, Beam relies on Apple frameworks that, by design, process data on Apple's infrastructure under Apple's privacy terms — including Maps, Location, and AR. The developer does not see, log, or persist the data or results of these calls beyond their immediate use in the app. See Apple's privacy policy for how Apple handles this data.

Permissions

Beam may ask for permission to use:

  • Camera — to show the live camera feed behind the AR view.
  • Location — to compute the sun's path for your position and bias map searches toward nearby places.

You can deny or revoke either permission in iOS Settings; the affected features will be unavailable but the rest of Beam keeps working.

Data stored on your device

Beam keeps a small amount of state on your device only — your preferences, onboarding progress, and recent searches. None of this is transmitted off the device by Beam. Deleting Beam removes all of it.

Children's privacy

Beam is suitable for all ages. The developer does not knowingly collect any data about anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

Material changes will be communicated via the App Store description or in a future update of this document. The "Last updated" header above reflects the most recent revision.

Contact

Questions about privacy can be sent to hello@beam.app.
